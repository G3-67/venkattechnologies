Working From Home Has Changed Everything

Working From Home Has Changed Everything:

COVID-19 has been a part of our lives for almost two years. Those two years fundamentally altered the way we work. Some people continue to work from home (WFH), while others have moved to a hybrid work schedule. Full-time office work is becoming a possibility for some, but it is unlikely to become the norm. Working from home has changed almost everything we thought we knew about work, posing challenges for both leaders and employees.

Creating the future workplace

The impact of the shift to remote working was discussed at the 2021 Drucker Forum, held in Vienna, Austria, with some delegates in person and others via the now-familiar Zoom platform. Andrew Hill, Associate Editor of the Financial Times, moderated a panel discussion titled "How Work From Home Has Changed Everything." He began by asking delegates about their own WFH experiences and how they saw the future of work evolving..

Leadership, empathy, and trust

Working from home provides a myriad of distractions. Home-schooling, childcare, and other insignificant tasks can disrupt traditional work schedules.

Defining Work in the Post-Pandemic Era

The workplace evolution triggered by the pandemic is still in its early stages. All of the panellists on this Drucker Forum panel agreed that the future would be hybrid, but it is unclear what that would look like for different organisations.
